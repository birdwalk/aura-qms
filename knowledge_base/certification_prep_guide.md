# ISO 9001:2015 Certification Preparation Guide (Synthetic — Demo Only)

## Certification Audit Process

### Stage 1 Audit (Documentation Review)
The external auditor reviews your documented QMS to confirm:
- The scope is adequately defined
- Required documented information exists
- The QMS structure addresses all applicable clauses

### Stage 2 Audit (Implementation Review)
The external auditor verifies that the QMS is effectively implemented:
- Interviews process owners on their clause responsibilities
- Reviews records and evidence of QMS operation
- Observes processes in action
- Raises findings (NCs, observations)

## Common Audit Failure Points
- Missing mandatory documented information (especially Clause 8.1 operational plans)
- Process owners unable to explain their clause responsibilities
- Records of training effectiveness not maintained
- Supplier evaluations not conducted or records incomplete
- Internal audit programme not followed or results not acted upon
- Management review not conducted or outputs not recorded

## Preparation Checklist
- [ ] All mandatory documented information present and approved
- [ ] All process owners trained and able to demonstrate clause understanding
- [ ] Internal audit completed within 12 months
- [ ] All previous NCs closed with verified effectiveness
- [ ] Management review conducted with all required inputs
- [ ] KPIs defined, monitored, and reported
- [ ] Supplier evaluations current for all critical suppliers
- [ ] Corrective action procedure understood by all process owners

## Assessment Pass Thresholds
- Process owner knowledge assessment: ≥80% to achieve certification readiness
- Department readiness score: ≥70% minimum across all departments
- No open major nonconformities at time of external audit
