# Corrective Action Procedure Guide (Synthetic — Demo Only)

## Purpose
To describe the process for identifying, investigating, and resolving nonconformities
and preventing their recurrence within the ISO 9001:2015 QMS.

## When to Raise a Corrective Action
- Any identified nonconformity (major or minor)
- Customer complaints related to product or service quality
- Process failures that result in non-conforming outputs
- Internal audit findings
- Management review outputs

## Root Cause Analysis Methods
- **5-Why Analysis**: Ask "why" five times to trace from symptom to root cause
- **Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa)**: Map causes across People, Process, Equipment, Environment, Material, Measurement
- **Pareto Analysis**: Identify the 20% of causes responsible for 80% of issues

## Corrective Action Steps
1. **Contain**: Immediate actions to stop the nonconformity from spreading
2. **Investigate**: Root cause analysis using appropriate method
3. **Plan**: Define corrective actions, responsibilities, and target dates
4. **Implement**: Execute the planned actions with evidence collection
5. **Verify**: Confirm effectiveness — did the root cause get eliminated?
6. **Close**: Close the finding with evidence of verification

## Timeframes
- Major nonconformity: Initial response within 5 working days; full CA plan within 15 days
- Minor nonconformity: Full CA plan within 30 days
- OFI: Addressed within agreed timeframe at management discretion

## Records Required
- Description of nonconformity
- Root cause analysis documentation
- Corrective action plan with responsibilities and dates
- Implementation evidence
- Effectiveness verification record
